# PRODUCT.md — SIGNAL v2 Portfolio

## What this is
Personal portfolio of Chaitanya Tripathi (ctport2026.vercel.app). A scroll-driven,
five-chapter narrative site whose organizing metaphor is *signal in noise*: a
holographic wavefront resolves out of static as you scroll, mirroring his research
(computational holography) and his builder thesis (making messy systems legible).

## Register
brand — the design IS the product. The site's job is to make a recruiter, founder,
or admissions officer stop and think "this person operates at a different level."

## Users
- Internship recruiters and startup founders (June–August 2026 window), skimming
  on a laptop from a link in an email or LinkedIn, usually at night, 60 seconds
  of attention unless something earns more.
- Researchers/professors evaluating the HoloForge working paper.
- Peers and collaborators.

## Voice and tone
First-person, precise, a little literary. Honest to a fault: metrics are real or
omitted, in-progress work is labeled in-progress. Confidence without inflation.

## Brand system
- Identity: "SIGNAL". Signal green (#00FF94) on near-black, shifting to phase
  blue (#5E8CDB) in the Research chapter.
- Type: Clash Display (display), Instrument Sans (body), mono for telemetry.
- Motifs: dot-matrix wavefront interference, scramble-to-decode text, grain,
  telemetry-style eyebrow labels (01 — Signal), the pulsing availability dot.

## Anti-references
- Generic SaaS landing pages, template portfolios (Notion/Webflow default look).
- Overclaiming: no fake metrics, no "AI-powered" fluff.
- Cream/minimal editorial portfolios — this identity is committed dark.

## Strategic principles
1. First five seconds must stun (loader → hero is the crown moment).
2. Every decorative system must reinforce the signal/wavefront metaphor.
3. Honesty is a feature: "what didn't work" sections stay.
4. Motion is scroll-driven and physical, never decorative bounce.
